PAW Chain Glossary

The PAW Chain Glossary of Terms provides definitions for key concepts, blockchain terminology, and PAW Chain-specific mechanisms. Whether you are a developer, validator, or community member, this glossary will help you navigate the PAW Chain ecosystem and its multichain capabilities.

This glossary includes terms related to staking, validation, governance, security, and multichain interoperability, ensuring a clear understanding of how PAW Chain operates and interacts with the broader crypto space.

  • Active Validator ‚Äì A validator that has met the required stake and is actively participating in block validation.
  • AMA (Ask Me Anything) ‚Äì A community engagement event where the PAW Chain team answers questions from the public.
  • Arbitrum (ARB) ‚Äì A Layer 2 scaling solution for Ethereum that PAW Chain supports for multichain deployments.
  • Avalanche (AVAX) ‚Äì A high-performance blockchain supported by PAW Chain for multichain functionality.
  • All-time high / All-time low (ATH/ATL)¬† ‚Äì This term refers to the highest or lowest price ever reached by a given asset.
  • Altcoins ‚Äì An altcoin refers to any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin. Each altcoin has its own set of rules, properties, and specific use cases. They may introduce new technologies or be forks of existing cryptocurrencies.

B‚Äã

  • Base (BASE) ‚Äì A Layer 2 Ethereum blockchain supported by PAW Chain.
  • Bear market ‚Äì¬† A bear market is the opposite of a bull market, where outlooks are negative as market prices trend downward.
  • Block ‚Äì A collection of transactions that are grouped together and added to the blockchain.
  • Blockchain ‚Äì A blockchain is a decentralized method of storing data, where information is duplicated and distributed across a network of computer systems. Operated by a community of miners or validators, blockchains function without a centralized intermediary.¬† Each block contains a cryptographic reference to the previous one, making it impossible to alter the blockchain's history.
  • Block Reward ‚Äì When miners or validators create new blocks, they receive a block reward in the form of newly minted cryptocurrency. This incentivizes participants to help maintain the blockchain.
  • BNB Chain (BNB) ‚Äì A blockchain network supported by PAW Chain for multichain deployment.
  • Bridge ‚Äì A mechanism that allows assets to be transferred between PAW Chain and other blockchain networks.
  • Bull market ‚Äì A bull market describes a period when market prices are generally trending upward and public perception is positive.
  • Burn ‚Äì The process of permanently removing tokens from circulation to reduce supply.

C‚Äã

  • Celo (CELO) ‚Äì A blockchain supported by PAW Chain, optimized for mobile payments and DeFi applications.
  • Checkpoints ‚Äì Also known as an "epoch," is a scheduled event that occurs every 12 hours to validate transactions and update network state.¬† At these checkpoints, rewards are given out to the Validators and Delegators when a block is signed. If a block is missed by the Validator, then the reward isn't distributed.
  • Consensus ‚Äì In cryptocurrencies, consensus refers to the method by which blockchain participants agree on what should be included in the blockchain. The most prominent consensus mechanisms are proof-of-work and proof-of-stake.
  • Cooldown Period ‚Äì Also known as "Unbonding," refers to the period one must wait from the time they un-stake with a Validator to the time their assets appear in their wallet. This length of time will be predetermined by the development team. There are both detriments and benefits to short Cooldown periods and long Cooldown periods.
  • Cronos (CRO) ‚Äì A blockchain supported by PAW Chain for multichain interactions.
  • Cross-Stitching ‚Äì PAW Chain‚Äôs methodology for selecting and verifying validators across multiple levels to enhance security.
  • Cryptography ‚Äì Cryptography is the science of keeping information secure. It is used to secure blockchains and cryptocurrencies through algorithms like SHA-256 for proof-of-work and private-key/public-key cryptography for validating transfers.
  • Cryptographic Hash ‚Äì A unique digital fingerprint of data used in blockchain transactions.

D‚Äã

  • DAO ‚Äì A Decentralized Autonomous Organization, or DAO, is a business entity represented by transparent rules encoded in smart contracts. It aims to reduce centralization, typically allowing the community to govern its future through voting.
  • dApp (Decentralized Application) ‚Äì An application built on blockchain technology without centralized control.¬† They are programs that run on blockchain networks using smart contracts to provide trustless tools and services. Ethereum is the most widely adopted platform for dApps.
  • Decentralized ‚Äì Decentralized technologies use distributed systems to provide increased security and redundancy while reducing reliance on governing bodies and centralized intermediaries.
  • Decentralized Exchange (DEX) ‚Äì A peer-to-peer marketplace for trading cryptocurrencies without intermediaries.
  • DeFi ‚Äì Decentralized Finance, or DeFi, is an ecosystem of applications and services that leverage blockchain technology to provide decentralized financial services, such as borrowing, lending, and earning interest on cryptocurrencies.
  • Delegator ‚Äì A user who stakes PAW tokens with a validator to earn a share of block rewards, thereby participating in the network's consensus process indirectly. By delegating their tokens/coins, they earn a share of the rewards generated by the validator for securing and validating transactions on the network.
  • Downtime Penalization ‚Äì The penalty imposed on validators for prolonged offline periods.
  • DYOR ‚Äì "Do Your Own Research." This warning from cryptocurrency influencers reminds investors that due diligence is crucial, as media outlets and influencers may have different incentives.

E‚Äã

  • ERC-20 ‚Äì An ERC-20 token is a widely used crypto-token standard on the Ethereum network, enabling developers to create digital currencies compatible with existing infrastructure.
  • ERC-721 ‚Äì The ERC-721 standard on the Ethereum network is used for Non-Fungible Tokens (NFTs), which are unique and cannot be counterfeited. They are used for digital collectibles, gaming items, and tokenizing real-world unique items.
  • EVM (Ethereum Virtual Machine) ‚Äì¬† EVM is a global blockchain-based computer that provides a runtime environment for creating decentralized applications on the Ethereum network as well as allowing smart contracts to execute on PAW Chain
  • Ethereum (ETH) ‚Äì A blockchain supported by PAW Chain for multichain functionality.

F‚Äã

  • Fantom (FTM) ‚Äì A blockchain network supported by PAW Chain, known for its fast and low-cost transactions.
  • Fiat ‚Äì Fiat refers to traditional money, such as the US dollar and the euro. Using fiat currencies in blockchain requires trusting a centralized entity to custody your funds.
  • Finality ‚Äì The point at which a transaction is confirmed and cannot be reversed.
  • First Come, First Served (FCFS) ‚Äì The process by which validator slots and lockers are allocated.
  • FOMO ‚Äì "Fear of missing out." In crypto markets, it refers to the anxiety of not owning tokens that are experiencing significant upward price movements.
  • Fork ‚Äì A fork occurs when a blockchain undergoes a protocol change, creating two parallel chains. Hard forks result in new currencies and break backward compatibility, while soft forks update rules and require support from the majority of participants.
  • FUD ‚Äì "Fear, uncertainty, and doubt." This refers to information that creates a pessimistic market outlook.
  • Fundamental analysis ‚Äì This method evaluates the value and future performance of an asset by considering factors like technology, team, and token distribution.

G‚Äã

  • Gas Fee ‚Äì The transaction fee paid to validators for processing transactions on the network.
  • Gnosis (GNO) ‚Äì A blockchain supported by PAW Chain for multichain interoperability.
  • Gwei ‚Äì A Gwei is a small unit of Ether, the native currency of Ethereum. One Ether equals 1,000,000,000 Gwei.

H‚Äã

  • Halving ‚Äì The reduction of the Bitcoin block reward by 50%. It occurs approximately every four years, with the current reward at 6.25 BTC per block.
  • Hard Fork ‚Äì A major blockchain upgrade that results in a new chain diverging from the old one.
  • Hashrate ‚Äì A measure of the computational power used for mining or validating transactions, indicating how many calculations are performed per second. A higher hash rate suggests a more secure network.
  • HODL ‚Äì A misspelling of "hold," defined as Hold On for Dear Life, referring to the practice of keeping cryptocurrencies instead of selling, often against market trends.

I‚Äã

  • Interoperability ‚Äì The ability of PAW Chain to interact with multiple blockchain networks.

K‚Äã

  • Keypair ‚Äì A cryptographic pair of public and private keys used to sign transactions.
  • KYC ‚Äì "Know Your Customer." The process by which financial service providers gather and verify customer information, enforced by governing bodies.

L‚Äã

  • Ledger ‚Äì A ledger is a database of transactions. In cryptocurrencies, it refers to the transaction history stored on the blockchain.
  • Limit order ‚Äì An order to buy or sell an asset at a specified price or better. A buy order executes at the target price or lower, and a sell order executes at the target price or higher.
  • Liquidity ‚Äì The availability with which a cryptocurrency can be bought or sold. It also refers to the amount of cryptocurrencies available for trading in a liquidity pool.
  • Linea (LINA) ‚Äì A Layer 2 blockchain connected to PAW Chain.
  • Locker ‚Äì An entity that provides additional staking support to validators who do not meet the full staking requirement.
  • Locker Yield ‚Äì The rewards earned by lockers for contributing to a validator‚Äôs stake.

M‚Äã

  • Mainnet ‚Äì The live, operational version of PAW Chain where real transactions take place.
  • Margin trading ‚Äì A trading method that involves using borrowed funds, allowing traders to leverage positions for greater potential profits and higher risks.
  • Market cap ‚Äì Market capitalization. The total value of an asset, calculated by multiplying its price by its circulating supply. It measures a project's success.
  • Multichain ‚Äì The ability of a project to exist and operate on multiple blockchain networks, including PAW Chain.
  • Matic (Polygon - MATIC) ‚Äì A layer 2 sidechain network supported by PAW Chain for multichain deployment.
  • MEV (Maximal Extractable Value) ‚Äì Profits extracted by validators through transaction ordering, reordering, or censorship.
  • Mining ‚Äì The process of using computing resources to create new blocks on a blockchain, earning rewards in return.
  • Mining pool ‚Äì A group of miners who combine their computing power to increase their chances of earning mining rewards.

N‚Äã

  • NFT ‚Äì Non-Fungible Tokens are unique digital assets on the Ethereum blockchain, used for digital collectibles, artworks, and gaming items.
  • Network fees ‚Äì Fees required to process transactions on the Bitcoin or Ethereum network, incentivizing participants to maintain the network.
  • Node ‚Äì A computer that participates in the PAW Chain network that stores data and contributes to the consensus process. Miners act as full nodes, but anyone can operate a node.
  • Non-Custodial ‚Äì A system where users maintain control over their private keys and funds without relying on a third party.
  • Nonce ‚Äì A random number used in proof-of-work blockchains like Bitcoin to solve cryptographic puzzles, allowing miners to add new blocks and earn rewards.

O‚Äã

  • Optimism (OP) ‚Äì A Layer 2 scaling solution for Ethereum supported by PAW Chain.

P‚Äã

  • Paper wallet ‚Äì A type of cryptocurrency wallet stored on paper to reduce private key exposure. While considered "cold storage," they are now seen as less secure due to potential centralization and GUI vulnerabilities.
  • PAW ($PAW) ‚Äì The native cryptocurrency of PAW Chain used for staking, validating, and rewards.
  • PAW Chain ‚Äì A blockchain network focused on decentralization, multichain interoperability, and validator participation.
  • PawJS ‚Äì PAW Chain is upgrading its Layer 1 infrastructure with PawJS, a new scripting language designed to be more user-friendly and efficient than Solidity. Set to launch in phase 3, PawJS will streamline smart contract and dApp development with intuitive syntax, enhanced readability, and robust features. This upgrade aims to boost developer participation, improve network performance, and empower the PAW Chain community, positioning the network for future scalability and innovation.
  • Partial Stake Validator ‚Äì A validator that has only a portion of the required 2.5T PAW staked and relies on lockers for the remaining stake.
  • Pending Pool ‚Äì A pool where validators who do not meet the staking requirement wait for additional support.
  • Penalization ‚Äì The process of fining validators for downtime or network inefficiencies.
  • Private key ‚Äì A password-like string that identifies the owner of a cryptocurrency wallet, allowing access to funds.
  • Proof of Authority Validators (PoA) ‚Äì PoA is a consensus mechanism used in blockchain networks where a limited number of pre-approved nodes, known as validators, are responsible for validating transactions and creating new blocks. Validators are chosen based on their reputation and identity, ensuring a high level of trust and security. This method reduces the risk of malicious activity and allows for faster transaction processing compared to other mechanisms like Proof of Work (PoW) or Proof of Stake (PoS).
  • Proof of Stake (PoS) ‚Äì A consensus mechanism where participants stake cryptocurrency to validate transactions and create new blocks, earning rewards.
  • Proof-of-work - A consensus mechanism using computing resources to solve cryptographic puzzles, allowing miners to create new blocks and earn rewards.
  • Public key - A long string of characters generated alongside a private key, used to produce a wallet address.

Q‚Äã

  • Quorum ‚Äì The minimum number of validators required to validate a block on the network.

R‚Äã

  • Rewards Pool ‚Äì The total pool of rewards distributed to validators, lockers, and delegators.
  • Rollups ‚Äì A Layer 2 scaling solution that bundles multiple transactions into a single batch for efficiency.

S‚Äã

  • Self-Stake ‚Äì The amount of PAW a validator must personally stake to be eligible for block validation.
  • Slashing ‚Äì When a certain Validator behaves poorly or deliberately, the operator (i.e., Validator) and each of its Delegators are penalized by a condition known as Slashing. Both the funds of the Validator and its Delegators are subject to penalties based on the amount staked. If a Validator gets slashed multiple times, eventually they get removed from the blockchain.
  • Smart Contract ‚Äì A self-executing contract stored and operated on blockchain networks, enabling decentralized applications to provide transparent financial tools and services.
  • Snapshot ‚Äì A recorded state of the blockchain at a specific point in time.
  • Solana (SOL) ‚Äì A blockchain that is connected to PAW Chain for multichain transactions.
  • Solidity ‚Äì A programming language used to write smart contracts, created by Ethereum co-founder Gavin Wood.
  • Staking ‚Äì The process of locking up PAW tokens to participate in network security and earn rewards.

T‚Äã

  • TA or technical analysis ‚Äì The study of price charts and market indicators to determine market trends and predict future movements.
  • Team Nodes ‚Äì Nodes operated by the PAW Chain team to ensure stability in the early phases of the blockchain.
  • Testnet ‚Äì A testing environment for smart contract platforms like Ethereum, allowing developers to test contracts with valueless cryptocurrencies.
  • Token ‚Äì Digital assets issued on a blockchain via smart contracts. Many top cryptocurrencies are Ethereum tokens.
  • Transaction Fee ‚Äì The cost associated with executing transactions on PAW Chain.
  • TVL (Total Value Locked) ‚Äì The total value of assets staked or locked in DeFi protocols.

U‚Äã

  • Unbonding ‚Äì The process of removing a stake from a validator or locker, subject to a 7-day unlock period.
  • Unlock Period ‚Äì The duration required to unstake tokens from a validator or locker.
  • UniChain (UNI) ‚Äì A blockchain supported by PAW Chain for multichain deployments.

V‚Äã

  • Validator ‚Äì A node that participants in proof-of-stake blockchains who verify transactions, maintain network integrity, and earn rewards by securing the PAW Chain network.
  • Validator Pool ‚Äì The total number of validators available to participate in block validation.
  • Volatility ‚Äì The tendency of an asset's price to vary, often seen in young markets like cryptocurrencies.
  • Volume ‚Äì The total trading activity of an asset within a given period.

W‚Äã

  • Wallet ‚Äì Tools used by crypto holders to control private keys and interface with the blockchain. Wallets can be software or hardware-based, custodial or non-custodial.
  • Whale ‚Äì A term for major players in the cryptocurrency markets, such as institutional investors, hedge funds, or wealthy individuals.
  • Whitelisting ‚Äì The process of approving projects or addresses to participate in specific network functions.
  • White paper ‚Äì Documents proposing new technologies, outlining details of their implementation. White papers help users and investors understand a project, its use case, and potential.
  • wPAW (Wrapped PAW) ‚Äì A tokenized version of PAW that can be used on other blockchains.
